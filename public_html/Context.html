Tim Berners-Lee

A physicist Tim Berners-Lee in Switzerland in Cern. He developed a software to more easily follow the threads of knowledge within the information stored throughout the Internet. He named his invention a world wide web. In 1992, there were only 50 web pages on it.

In 1993 at the University of Illinois, a 22 year-old student Marc Adreessen invented Netscape. It enables the access to the world wide web and let people have easier way to access to the world wide web. The browser can show text, scroll bars, jump around the web when clicking the mouse ensure it was user friendly. It wasn’t the first or the last browser, but it was a breakthrough application. It brought the fire of web into the humankind.

In the beginning of 21 century, a lot of things came online. At present there are over 11000000 (11M) domain names registered, nearly 70000000 (70M) websites, www, .com, @ are the new icon for information age
